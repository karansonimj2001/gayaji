Pind Daan & Shradh in Gaya Ji

Gaya, a city deeply revered by Hindus and Buddhists alike, is a spiritual beacon in Bihar, India.

Vishnu Pad: The Sacred Footprint of Lord Vishnu

In the heart of the revered Vishnu Pad temple lies the sacred footprint of Lord Vishnu, a symbol cherished in Hinduism.

Falgu River: Sacred Waters of Gaya

The Falgu River, flowing through Gaya, is considered sacred and attracts pilgrims for ritual baths.

Bodh Gaya: Where Enlightenment Was Attained

Bodh Gaya is known as the site where Gautama Buddha attained enlightenment under the Bodhi tree.

Pretshila Hill: The Sacred Hill for Pind Daan

Pretshila Hill is an important site in Gaya for performing pind daan rituals.

Akshayabat: The Eternal Banyan Tree

Akshayabat is a sacred banyan tree in Gaya, symbolizing eternal life and protection.

Barabar Caves: Ancient Rock-Cut Sanctuaries

The Barabar Caves in Gaya are considered the oldest rock-cut caves in India.

Surya Kund: Sacred Pond of the Sun God

Surya Kund is a sacred pond dedicated to the Sun God, where devotees gather for blessings.
